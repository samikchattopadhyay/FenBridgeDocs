Workflow Module 6

Rejection Handling

Process lot rejections during verification with disposition options. Scout documents rejection reason with coded categories and defect evidence. Determines if lot is reconditionable (path to re-inspection) or terminal (voided from market).

Rejection Disposition Steps
6 Steps
Step 1Rejection Initiation
Trigger
Scout detected defect during verification or seal application that prevents lot from transacting.
Context
Scout must provide reason and document with evidence photos. Rejection recorded to lot audit trail.
Step 2Rejection Reason Selection
Coded Categories
  • Quality Defect (color, smell, texture, foreign matter)
  • Storage Failure (moisture, pest signs, mold, contamination)
  • Seal Integrity Issue (broken seal, tampering signs, net damage)
  • Documentation Mismatch (grade claim vs actual, quantity variance >10%)
  • Non-Compliance (regulatory, traceability, missing certifications)
  • Environmental Damage (water damage, exposure, packaging failure)
Step 3Defect Evidence & Notes
Evidence Photos
Minimum 2-3 mandatory defect close-ups showing affected product. Geo-stamped, ≤1.5MB each.
Description
Optional detailed notes (≤500 chars) explaining severity and affected portion of lot.
Step 4Reconditionability Assessment
Reconditionable Path
  • Defect is partial (affects portion of lot, not entire)
  • Remediation is feasible (re-grade, re-bag, re-clean)
  • Post-reconditioning re-inspection possible within 7 days
  • Example: partial moisture damage in sealed lot → dry out, re-inspect
Terminal Path
  • Defect is total (entire lot compromised)
  • Remediation not feasible (pest infestation, contamination, severe mold)
  • Lot cannot return to market; no re-inspection pathway
  • Example: sealed lot with rodent infestation → void immediately
Step 5Disposition Decision
Reconditionable Decision
  • Scout marks lot status: rejected_pending_reconditioning
  • Creates re-inspection task scheduled for 7 days out
  • Farmer/popper notified to recondition lot
  • On re-inspection success: lot status restored to previous state (sealed/active)
  • On re-inspection fail: lot voided immediately (irreversible)
Terminal Decision
  • Scout marks lot status: voided (irreversible)
  • Notifies farmer/popper immediately
  • Lot removed from all marketplaces (spot offers voided, PO cancelled)
  • Financial settlement per contract terms (if active PO)
Step 6Notification & Recording
Notifications
  • Scout receives confirmation of rejection recorded
  • Farmer/popper alerted with rejection reason and photos
  • Buyer notified if active PO (status change to cancelled)
  • Admin sees rejection in audit trail with full photo + notes evidence
Record & Sync
Rejection record queued to sync_queue. All photos, reason, evidence persisted to lot audit trail. Record immutable; no scout editing allowed.

Rejection Workflow & Next Steps

Recondition Pathway
Rejected → Pending Reconditioning → Re-Inspect (success: Restore status, fail: Void). Max 7-day recondition window.
Terminal Pathway
Voided immediately. Farmer loses revenue. No appeal pathway; Admin review only for edge cases.
Market Impact
Active spot offers voided. Active POs cancelled with settlement. Sealed lots marked void in all marketplaces.
Immutability
Rejection record cannot be edited by scout. Full evidence chain preserved in audit trail for accountability.
